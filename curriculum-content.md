## PAGE 5: What We Work On (Day & Train)

**Day & Train — What Your Dog Learns**

**Essential Commands**
- Sit, Down, Stay, Come, Place
- Trained to reliability in real-world situations with distractions
- Marker-based communication so your dog understands exactly what's expected

**Calm on Command (Place)**
- Elevated place cots throughout the facility
- Your dog learns to go to their spot and settle — on command
- This is the #1 skill owners tell us changed their life at home

**Loose Leash Walking**
- Real-world leash manners, not just indoor compliance
- Practiced in our outdoor yard with real distractions
- Your dog walks beside you, not pulling you down the street

**Treadmill & Slatmill Fitness**
- Controlled cardio for high-energy dogs
- Builds focus, endurance, and impulse control
- Treadmill: motorized, controlled pace
- Slatmill: self-paced, your dog sets the speed

**Socialization**
- Structured play with temperament-matched groups
- Your dog practices social skills in a safe, supervised environment
- We don't just make dogs social — we keep them social for life

## PAGE 9: Spay/Neuter Policy

**A Note on Intact Dogs**

Spaying or neutering is **strongly recommended** but not required for daycare.

However, here's what we've learned from 15 years of experience:

**Intact males over 1 year** struggle significantly in a social daycare environment. They tend to become territorial, mark obsessively, and fixate on females. This makes it very difficult for them — and the dogs around them — to have a good time.

**Intact females** generally do better, but can still create disruption in group dynamics.

If your dog is intact, we recommend our **Day & Train program** where they receive individualized attention and structured management throughout the day. This gives them all the benefits of socialization with the hands-on support they need.

We're happy to discuss the best fit for your dog — just ask.

## PAGE 12: FAQ

**Frequently Asked Questions**

**What vaccinations are required?**
Rabies, bordetella, and DHPP — all must be current before your dog's first visit.

**Can intact dogs attend?**
Spay/neuter is strongly recommended. Intact males over 1 year struggle in group daycare — they tend to mark, mount, and become territorial. Intact females generally do better. We recommend Day & Train for intact dogs where they get individualized management.

**How often should my dog attend?**
For best results, we recommend 3–5 days per week — especially for the first few months. Consistency builds confidence and social skills.

**My dog is nervous — is that okay?**
Absolutely. We specialize in making nervous dogs confident. We recommend consistent attendance to build routine and lasting social skills.

**Do I need to bring food?**
No — unless you have a puppy and want to provide a lunch. We supply fresh water throughout the day.

**How do I know how my dog is doing?**
You'll receive a weekly report card covering your dog's behaviour, social progress, and any notes from our team.

**What training tools do you use?**
For Day & Train, we use prong collars, e-collars, slip leads, and muzzles as needed. The e-collar delivers TENS unit-type muscle stimulation — the same technology used by chiropractors — not a shock.

**What if my dog gets injured?**
All staff are trained in canine first aid. We maintain direct contact with nearby emergency vet clinics. In 15 years, we've maintained a perfect safety record.
