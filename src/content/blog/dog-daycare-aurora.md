Aurora is home to several franchise daycare operations — Dogtopia, Camp Bow Wow, and others. They're convenient. They're branded. And they all run essentially the same model: large open-play rooms, webcams, and high dog-to-staff ratios.

Here's what that model doesn't give you:

### Behaviour Expertise, Not Just Supervision

Our staff are canine behaviour specialists — not college students with a two-week orientation. At a franchise, the staff are trained to follow protocols. At Academy, the staff are trained to read dogs.

### Behavioural Daycare — Something Franchises Don't Offer

If your dog is reactive, anxious, over-aroused, or has been turned away from another daycare, you already know the franchise model doesn't work for them. Large open-play rooms with 20+ dogs are the worst possible environment for a dog that struggles with impulse control or social pressure.

Our Behavioural Daycare program ($95/day) puts these dogs in smaller, carefully managed groups with hands-on trainer guidance throughout the day. It's a program that simply doesn't exist at franchise locations — because it requires the kind of expertise they don't staff for.

### Day & Train — Training Without Giving Up Your Weekends

Our Day & Train program ($95/day) combines full obedience training with daycare. Your dog gets 1-on-1 training sessions — commands, leash work, treadmill fitness, place cot training — woven between structured socialization with temperament-matched groups.

Instead of dragging yourself to a Saturday morning group class in a parking lot, your dog gets trained by professionals while you're at work. They come home tired, better behaved, and making real progress week over week.

This program is especially valuable for intact dogs. Spay and neuter aren't required at Academy, but intact males over 1 year often do better with the individualized structure that Day & Train provides rather than open group play.

### Temperament Matching, Not Size Sorting

Most franchise daycares sort dogs by size — small room, big room, done. That's a blunt instrument. A timid 60-pound Lab has nothing in common with a pushy 60-pound Pitbull mix, but they'd end up in the same group.

We match dogs by temperament, energy level, and play style. Your dog plays with dogs that are genuinely compatible, which means better socialization, less stress, and no bullying.
